🛒 Temu Business Strategy Analysis

How Temu Built a $70 Billion Business in Three Years

Project Overview

Temu entered the United States in September 2022 and rapidly became one of the fastest-growing e-commerce platforms in the world. Within three years, the company expanded from a new market entrant into a global marketplace generating an estimated $70.8 billion in Gross Merchandise Value (GMV).

This project investigates the business strategy behind that growth by combining Python, SQL, and Power BI to answer one central question:

«Did Temu build a sustainable business, or did it simply buy market share through aggressive spending?»

Using publicly available market estimates, this analysis explores Temu's growth strategy, advertising efficiency, unit economics, competitive positioning, and the impact of the 2025 US tariff changes.

---

Business Questions

This project answers several key business questions:

- How quickly did Temu scale after launch?
- Was advertising driving sustainable growth?
- Did Temu generate enough revenue to justify its acquisition costs?
- When did the company shift its focus from the United States to Europe?
- How does Temu compare with Amazon, Shein, AliExpress and Wish?
- Can Temu's current business model remain sustainable after the 2025 tariff changes?

Key Findings

- Temu increased estimated GMV from $275 million to $70.8 billion within three years.
- Customer acquisition was primarily driven by unprecedented advertising investment.
- Early growth depended heavily on subsidized pricing and aggressive marketing.
- Advertising efficiency improved as the platform matured.
- The 2025 US tariff changes significantly affected Temu's US strategy.
- Advertising investment shifted rapidly from the United States toward Europe.
- Despite remarkable growth, long-term profitability remains uncertain due to aggressive customer acquisition spending.

---

Business Insights

This project suggests that Temu's strategy focused on purchasing market share rather than maximizing short-term profitability.

Strong backing from PDD Holdings enabled Temu to sustain significant losses while rapidly expanding its customer base.

The long-term success of this strategy depends on whether customer lifetime value ultimately exceeds acquisition costs.

Key Takeaway

Temu's remarkable expansion was not solely driven by low prices. It was powered by an aggressive customer acquisition strategy supported by substantial advertising investment and strong financial backing from PDD Holdings.

Whether this strategy proves sustainable after the reduction in US expansion and the shift toward Europe remains one of the company's most important strategic questions.
