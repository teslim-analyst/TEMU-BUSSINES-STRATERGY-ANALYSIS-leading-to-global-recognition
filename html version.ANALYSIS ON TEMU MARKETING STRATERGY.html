Temu Business Strategy Analysis How Did Temu Build a $70 Billion Business in 3 Years? **Tools:** Python · SQL · Power BI **Data Sources:** Business of Apps · Backlinko · MobiLoud · PDD Holdings Investor Disclosures **Data Type:** Manually compiled public market estimates (Q4 2022 – Q2 2025) Background Temu debuted in the United States in September 2022 as a highly affordable e-commerce service provided by Chinese multinational company PDD Holdings. It took less than a year and a half for the app to be installed over 300 million times worldwide, become the most downloaded shopping app in America by displacing Shein, and spend an estimated $3 billion in ads within one year. The central question that will be investigated here is if such incredible growth has any business sense, or is there something else going on. Scope of This Project - Quarterly GMV and user growth since launch to mid 2025 - Advertising spending efficiency on US and EU markets - Unit economics analysis – comparing revenues and costs per user - Competitive positioning relative to Amazon, Shein, AliExpress and Wish - Effect of 2025 US tariff increase on Temu's business model and

Summary of the Project — What We Learned from the Data Temu opened its doors in the US in September 2022 and generated $275 million in GMV. This figure went up to $28 billion at the end of 2024. This level of growth does not come out of nowhere. You achieve that growth if you are ready to invest money you have not yet earned. That is precisely what they did. For almost all of their early quarters, Temu spent about as much in advertising as they made in revenue. Just Meta advertising in 2023 cost them $2 billion. In 2024, they ran three Super Bowl advertisements in one day. Every chart we can draw in this notebook leads us to one conclusion: Temu was not building a sustainable business, they were purchasing market share and betting on it that customers would stay long enough for them to be right. The data says that the bets are still open. Their average order value went up from $24 to $46 over the whole period of time. That means growth was driven exclusively by the sheer volume of newly acquired customers, not by the increased spending of their current users.
